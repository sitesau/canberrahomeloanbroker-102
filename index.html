Home Loan Broker Canberra Costs Explained

The majority of residential mortgage brokers in Australia do not charge the borrower a direct fee for their services. Instead, they are remunerated by the lender that settles the loan. This payment typically consists of an upfront commission, calculated as a percentage of the loan amount, followed by a trailing commission paid over the life of the loan. This model incentivises the broker to place the client with a suitable lender that will approve the loan and service the account well. When engaging a home loan broker canberra residents can often access this expertise without an out-of-pocket expense for the brokerage itself.

By accessing a panel of over 100 lenders, brokers can compare products that are not always available directly to consumers. This competition helps secure competitive interest rates and features that suit the borrower's financial situation. The service covers the entire application process, from the initial discussion through to settlement, handling the paperwork and lender liaison on the borrower's behalf.

Understanding Direct Fees and the Credit Guide

While the service is often free for standard residential loans, borrowers should be aware that direct fees can apply in specific circumstances. ASIC requires brokers to provide a credit guide before giving credit assistance. This document must disclose the broker's remuneration structure, including any commissions or fees that the borrower might pay. Some brokers may charge a fee for complex investment lending or commercial loans.

It is vital to read this guide to understand if there are any costs associated with your specific situation, ensuring there are no surprises when the application is submitted. The credit guide must also include complaint-handling details and AFCA contact information, ensuring the borrower has recourse if something goes wrong. Always verify who holds the Australian credit licence covering the broker.

The Cost Impact of Lenders Mortgage Insurance

One of the most significant costs for home buyers with a smaller deposit is Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). This insurance protects the lender if the borrower defaults and is usually required when the deposit is less than 20 percent of the property value. LMI can add tens of thousands of dollars to the upfront cost of buying a home, effectively increasing the amount required to settle.

However, government schemes can mitigate this cost. The First Home Guarantee scheme allows eligible buyers to purchase with a 5 percent deposit without paying LMI. Alternatively, the Help to Buy scheme reduces the deposit requirement to around 2 percent through a government shared-equity arrangement, also avoiding the need for LMI. Using a broker who understands these schemes can result in substantial savings by structuring the application to qualify.

Reducing Purchase Costs with ACT Concessions

In addition to managing loan fees, brokers can help buyers navigate state-level taxes that impact the total cost of purchase. From 1 July 2026, the ACT Home Buyer Concession Scheme removes the conveyance duty burden for eligible buyers, with no property value cap or income threshold. Eligible buyers pay no duty on homes or vacant residential land, representing a massive upfront saving.

To qualify, buyers must not have owned property in Australia in the previous five years and must live in the home for at least 12 months starting within one year of settlement. Because the eligibility relies on precise timing of the contract and occupancy, professional advice is valuable. These duty savings, combined with a loan that avoids LMI, significantly reduce the financial barrier to entering the Canberra property market.
